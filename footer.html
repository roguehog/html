About Roguehog.

Roguehog was inspired by witnessing endless incidents of dangerous driving as well as the accompanying stress and feeling of helplessness and frustration as the offenders sped away… the widespread adoption of dashcams presented a game changing opportunity to have eyes everywhere!

We crowdsource actual dashcam footage of traffic incidents and violations to provide auto insurers and other organizations with real world data reports on the driving behavior of their clients – and reward dashcam users for their uploads. A win-win situation.

Further bonuses include the prospect of safer roads – drivers thinking twice before acting recklessly, knowing one their fellow road users could record and report them – and cheaper and fairer insurance, through more accurate risk assessments and fewer accidents.

Motor vehicle crashes cost the US nearly $1 trillion per year. And the effects are pervasive with 28 percent property damage, 25 percent workplace productivity loss, 8 percent household productivity loss, 39 percent medical costs, traffic congestion, and other costs.

Roguehog – with your participation – can play an important role in changing the landscape of our roads. Join us!

Frequently asked questions.

General

Where does Roguehog operate?

We currently operate only in the United States.

Is the personal information I provide secure?

Yes, Roguehog does not sell personal data.

Is it legal to collect and sell data and video footage which feature personal license plate numbers?

Yes, it is legal in the United States to collect and sell information on traffic violations and accidents.

How does Roguehog generate revenue?

We sell video reports to our customers.

Uploaders

What information needs to be included with video uploads?

We require a license plate, date and approximate time of the traffic violation, state, GPS coordinates, violation, description of violation, and clear video evidence.

What video format is required for uploading?

We accept all video formats. Uploaded videos are then converted to mp4 format.

What is the maximum video file size allowed?

We allow a maximum file size of 20MB, or 30 seconds of footage, to be uploaded.

Can I only use a dashcam to record video?

We require high-quality video footage, so a dashcam is preferable.

How much will I get paid for each report?

Video uploaders receive 40 percent of the revenue ($15) each time a report is sold.

How long will my report data and dashcam footage be stored on your system?

Roguehog will store the uploaded data indefinitely.

Do I get paid for every video I upload?

No, payments are made to uploaders only when video reports get purchased by users.

Customers

How much does Roguehog charge for the purchase of each video report?

We charge $25 per report.

How are payments for video reports made?

We use Stripe as payment processor.

How do I find traffic violation reports on Roguehog?

You can either search our database through the Roguehog app, or upload license plate numbers in question and then get notified automatically by email when a match is found in our database.
